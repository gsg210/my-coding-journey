About Me

I created this journal to chronicle my journey through the victories and frustrations of learning how to code.

Earlier this summer I inquired about a coding bootcamp and signed up for a 24-week course at UT Austin. I had entertained the idea of coding on and off, but I kept putting it off because it seemed so daunting. A lot of aspects still are; I'm really new to it! But the more I do it, the more things click.

This bootcamp has been the push I needed to finally get the ball rolling. I tend to thrive in a group setting where I can draw motivation and a sense of accountability. The same went for a fitness bootcamp I attended when my kids were little. That bootcamp was the final push I needed to get back to pre-baby shape.

Anyhow... I hope you enjoy this journal. My first entry was written three days after my first class, and as you'll see, some pages won't be written up in the same format.

As you'll notice in the first few entries, there's no way to navigate other than backing out of them, but I'm going to leave those design flaws in place as a way of tracking my progress throughout the program.

I've also included a few resources below that I was either introduced to in class or discovered on my own time. These have all been instrumental in my progress.
